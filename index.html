Regulated by Cayman Investment Monetary Authority

Envescom SPC is registered as an administered mutual fund with the Cayman Investment Monetary Authority (CIMA), a regulatory body with the object of protecting and enhancing the integrity of the financial services industry of the Cayman Islands. The Segregated Portfolio Company structure gives us a flexibility to register multiple funds as a segregated portfolio under one master fund. Each segregated portfolio is legally ring fenced and has no recourse to each other or the master fund.

CIMA regulates funds and fund administrators in accordance with the laws and regulations applicable to all regulated entities and those specifically governing this sector, namely, the Mutual Funds Law; Mutual Funds (Annual Returns) Regulations, 2006; and the Mutual Fund Administrators Licence (Applications) Regulations 2001; as well as the relevant rules, guidance, policies and procedures issued by the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority; relevant international standards set by the International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO) and the Offshore Group of Collective Investment Scheme Supervisors (OGCISS).
